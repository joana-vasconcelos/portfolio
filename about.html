About me

Front-end developer based in Lisbon, Portugal

Joana Vasconcelos

Since I was young all I wanted was to help biodiversity and the environment, so I thought Environmental Engineering would be best for me. It was mainly when I started working that I realised I didn't really care for this job. So I decided to take some time to think and try to understand what I wanted to do.

For a while I worked as a store operator, which helped me a lot socially and where I was part of a great team. It also allowed me to organise a lot of the store, which helped with my creativity and love for design and aesthetic.

Meanwhile, I applied for a Minor in Biology, while maintaining my job as a store operator. I then decided to apply for a Master's degree in Evolutionary and Developmental Biology. Suffice to say, I've loved the Evolutionary part of the course, but my favourite courses always involved a lot of computer use and some type of programming.

During the pandemic I've decided to expand my knowledge in programming, so I decided to start the SheCodes workshops, which have been a great learning tool that allowed me to know about HTML, CSS, JavaScript, Bootstrap, API, React and Responsive.

All of these experiences made me want to apply to a job in web development, while maintaining my love for biology and environment as a hobby.

My journey also made me realise that I'm always eager to learn new things. Right now I'm working on finishing my thesis and improve my coding skills. My next goals are to learn some Python and to start learning German.
